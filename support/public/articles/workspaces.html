Workspaces

Creating and managing workspaces

Last updated May 2025

Workspaces are the top-level containers in Todoer. Every task, file, and collaborator belongs to a workspace. You can have as many workspaces as you need — one per project, client, or team.

Creating a workspace

From the Workspaces page, click New workspace. You'll be prompted to enter:

  • Name — required, up to 64 characters
  • Description — optional, shown on the workspace overview

Once created, you'll be taken directly into the new workspace.

Workspace settings

Click the gear icon in the top-right corner of any workspace to open its settings. From here you can:

  • Rename the workspace or update its description
  • Manage members and their roles
  • Configure the public feed
  • Delete the workspace

Switching between workspaces

The workspace switcher lives in the top navigation bar. Click your current workspace name to open the dropdown and select another, or click Create new to add one.

Workspace roles

Every member of a workspace has one of two roles:

  • Owner — can manage settings, invite and remove members, and delete the workspace. The creator of a workspace is its owner by default.
  • Member — can view, add, and complete tasks, and upload files. Cannot change workspace settings or remove other members.

Ownership can be transferred to another member from the Members tab in workspace settings.

Deleting a workspace

Only the workspace owner can delete a workspace. Go to workspace settings and scroll to the bottom of the page. Deleting a workspace is permanent — all tasks, files, and conversation history associated with it are removed and cannot be recovered.

Warning Workspace deletion cannot be undone. Export or back up any files you need before proceeding.

Workspace limits

There is no hard limit on the number of workspaces you can create. Task and file limits depend on your plan. If you hit an unexpected limit, contact support.
